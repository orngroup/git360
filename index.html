Phase 1 · Pre-Sale & Market Strategy
Pre-Sale — Market Development & Product Launch

Before a single ticket is sold, GIT must have the product built, rates agreed, and the sales machine active. This phase runs 6–12 months ahead of any departure. Currently underdeveloped — strengthening this layer is critical to filling tours, reducing last-minute pressure, and improving margins across the board.

Seema · Sachin Keni

Sales · Supply · CEO India

Tour Design & Costing
  • Define itineraries by departure month (shoulder vs peak)
  • Build cost model: hotels, transport, sightseeing, TM fees
  • Set selling price with minimum 20% target margin
  • Create tour variants: standard, premium, FIT extensions
  • Publish to B2B portal and Indian agent network
  • Minimum group size of 20 pax to open a departure
12 months out

  • Agent incentives: early-bird group commission structure
Year-round

B2B Sales Enablement
  • Train agents on GIT product range and European itinerary USPs
  • Distribute collateral: brochures, rate cards, itinerary PDFs

Solving this upstream reduces last-minute booking chaos downstream for Nishanth and Clinton.

Phase 2 · Supply & Commercial Agreements
Contracting — Fixed Rates, Fixed Supply, No Surprises

The root cause of most operational failures is the absence of pre-contracted, fixed-rate supply. Tours are priced against hoped-for spot rates, and components are booked last-minute in search of savings that rarely materialise. Sachin Keni and Ajay Kawa lead all supply contracting with EK, European DMCs, hotels, transport and sightseeing partners. All key contracts must be signed by October for the following summer season — no exceptions.

Phase Owners
Sachin Keni · Ajay Kawa
Supply Lead · CCO · EK & Europe DMC Partners
02.1 · Hotels
Fixed Hotel Contracts

Annual fixed allotment contracts with all European hotels across GIT itineraries. Sachin Keni leads negotiations; Ajay Kawa signs off commercially.

  • Fixed net rate per hotel, per season
  • Allotment: minimum 2 rooms per departure date
  • Child and single supplement agreed upfront
  • Release policy: no penalty cancellation to T-14
  • Ken Newton scope: 24 core European hotels contracted
  • Signed contracts in place by October each year
Oct deadline
02.2 · Transport
Coach & Transfer Agreements

Fixed coach rates with Adonis and European transport partners per route and season. No ad-hoc quoting during live operations. Sachin Keni owns supplier relationship.

  • Rate per km/day by route (UK, France, Benelux, Alps)
  • 45-passenger cap written into contract — non-negotiable
  • Dual-coach rate agreed for groups exceeding 45 pax
  • Driver + TM room costs included in rate
  • 20-day advance notice clause for allocation changes
  • Adonis as primary ground transport partner
Oct deadline
02.3 · Sightseeing & EK
Attraction & Airline Agreements

Pre-purchase group ticket allotments for high-demand attractions. EK partnership managed by Ajay Kawa — group airfare agreements support GIT departure viability.

  • Disney Paris: group portal booking agreement in place
  • Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Colosseum: group entry agreements
  • Pink Strawberries / DMCs for specialist access
  • EK group fares: confirmed per season with Ajay Kawa
  • Allotments released 90 days before each departure
  • Cancellation terms: max 14 days without penalty
Oct deadline
02.4 · Tour Managers
TM Panel & ORN Connect

Contracted panel of experienced European TMs maintained year-round. Budgeted via ORN Connect at €750/service day. Sachin Keni manages the roster.

  • Retain minimum 8 TMs across UK and Europe
  • ORN Connect: 1 TM per 3 tours (internal coaching model)
  • TM allocated to tour at T-30, confirmed at T-20
  • TM briefing pack prepared by Clinton Thomas at T-7
  • Emergency TM cover protocol: Adonis + local contacts
  • TM debrief report mandatory within 5 days of return
Year-round panel
Critical Rule — No Spot-Rate Shopping: Once a supply contract is in place, no GIT team member may book outside that contract without written sign-off from Sachin Keni and Ajay Kawa. The practice of delaying bookings to find cheaper last-minute rates is prohibited from T-45 to T-0. All rate decisions must be made at contracting stage — not during live operations.

Phase 3 · Post-Sale Operations — The Critical Phase
Post-Sale — From Booking Received to Pre-Departure

This is where most current failures originate. The moment a passenger books, the clock starts. Nishanth D leads the overall operations function; Clinton Thomas owns the pre-departure countdown, QC process, and file sign-off. They work in close partnership — Nishanth providing operational oversight and escalation authority; Clinton executing every component check, booking confirmation, and TM briefing. Together they are accountable for zero surprises on departure day.

Joint Phase Owners
Nishanth D & Clinton Thomas
GIT Operations Head · Pre-Departure & QC Lead
03.1 · Booking Receipt
File Creation & Handler Assignment

Every booking triggers immediate file creation. Nishanth D assigns a named file handler within 24 hours. That individual owns all components on the file from this point through to departure.

  • Tour file created in system within 24hrs of booking
  • File handler named by Nishanth D — one person, one file
  • Passenger manifest: name, passport, visa status logged
  • Departure date, itinerary version, group size noted
  • Deposit confirmed; balance due date set at T-60
  • File status reviewed by Clinton Thomas at each T-gate
Nishanth D assigns
03.2 · Passenger Management
Manifest, Visas & Documentation

A live, accurate manifest is maintained from booking through departure. Visa-pending passengers are tracked separately by the file handler and reported to Clinton Thomas weekly.

  • Live manifest updated with every booking amendment
  • Visa status tracked: confirmed / pending / refused
  • Refused visa: refund per policy, file record closed
  • Full payment collected at T-60 — no exceptions
  • Special requirements: dietary, medical, mobility flagged
  • Weekly visa status report to Clinton Thomas
File handler owned
03.3 · Department Coordination
4-Team Internal Handoffs

Flights, Hotels, Transport and Sightseeing each receive the confirmed manifest and execute their bookings per the T-minus schedule. Clinton Thomas tracks every component status daily and escalates to Nishanth D if any gate is missed.

  • Flights: passenger sectors confirmed at T-45
  • Hotels: room allocation released and confirmed at T-45
  • Transport: Adonis notified directly by Clinton at T-20
  • Sightseeing: all tickets purchased by Clinton at T-14
  • Clinton tracks every open item daily on shared file
  • Nishanth D escalation authority for any missed gate
Clinton tracks daily

Phase 5 · Live Operations & Exception Management
On-Tour — Execution, Daily Reporting & Escalation

If the T-minus countdown is executed correctly, Phase 5 is low-drama. The Tour Manager runs the tour; Clinton Thomas is reachable from India during UK office hours; Nishanth D holds escalation authority for anything significant. Adonis handles transport; hotels have confirmed rooming lists. The protocols below cover what happens when something goes wrong anyway.

On-Ground Owner
Tour Manager
Clinton Thomas (India support) · Nishanth D (escalation)
05.1 · Daily Reporting
TM Daily Check-In

Tour Manager sends a brief daily update to Clinton Thomas by 8pm local time. Five lines maximum. Confirms next day's schedule, any issues, coach and hotel status. Clinton acknowledges within 2 hours.

  • Daily WhatsApp or email — no exceptions
  • Confirms: pax count, hotel status, next venue, coach
  • Any complaint or incident flagged immediately
  • Clinton acknowledges within 2 hours (India time)
  • Nishanth D copied on any issue raised
05.2 · Transport Protocol
Coach Capacity & Adonis

Hard maximum 45 passengers per coach at all times. TM counts pax before boarding every single day. Any vehicle change mid-tour requires Clinton and Nishanth D sign-off in advance.

  • TM pax count before boarding — daily, without exception
  • Luggage capacity checked before each departure
  • Vehicle change: Clinton + Nishanth D, 48hr minimum notice
  • Adonis is primary transport contact on the ground
  • No passenger left behind — ever. Escalate immediately.
05.3 · Sightseeing Protocol
Attraction Access & Tickets

All tickets are pre-booked and in the TM pack. TM presents booking references at every venue. No walk-up purchases. Any access refusal is escalated to Clinton Thomas immediately.

  • Booking references in TM pack (printed and digital)
  • Group entry lanes used at all pre-booked venues
  • If refused entry: call Clinton first, Adonis second
  • Any itinerary deviation requires Clinton approval
  • Nishanth D to be informed of any reroute within 1 hour
Escalation · Level 1
Venue Access Refused
  1. TM calls Clinton Thomas immediately
  2. Clinton contacts venue group bookings line
  3. If unresolved: identify nearest alternative venue
  4. Clinton approves reroute — Nishanth D informed
  5. Incident logged; refund claim initiated if applicable
Escalation · Level 2
Hotel Issue
  1. TM contacts Clinton Thomas with full details
  2. Clinton calls hotel directly on contracted number
  3. If unresolvable: source alternative rooms same area
  4. Cost difference: hotel bears the cost, not GIT
  5. Nishanth D notified; incident report filed within 24hrs
Escalation · Level 3
Medical / Emergency
  1. TM calls emergency services (112 across Europe)
  2. Immediate call to Clinton Thomas
  3. Clinton contacts passenger insurance provider
  4. Nishanth D and Sachin Keni notified within 30 mins
  5. Agent and family contacted by India office same hour

Phase 6 · Continuous Improvement
Post-Tour — Review, Reconcile & Improve

Every tour is a data point. The post-tour review closes the loop — capturing what worked, what didn't, and what changes before the next departure. Findings feed back into Phase 1 (product and pricing) and Phase 2 (contracting), making each season measurably better than the last. Clinton Thomas leads the review; Nishanth D reports findings to Sachin Keni and Ajay Kawa.

Review Owners
Clinton Thomas
Nishanth D
Reported to Sachin Keni & Ajay Kawa
06.1 · TM Debrief
Tour Manager Report

TM submits a structured debrief to Clinton Thomas within 5 days of tour completion. Non-negotiable. Forms the official record of the tour.

  • Hotels: quality, cleanliness, rooming issues
  • Transport: coach quality, driver performance
  • Sightseeing: access, timing, pax satisfaction
  • Incidents: complaints, escalations, resolutions
  • Overall pax sentiment score (1–10)
  • Supplier recommendations: renew or remove
06.2 · Finance Close
Cost Reconciliation

All supplier invoices reconciled against contracted rates within 10 days. Variances investigated. Tour P&L shared with Nishanth D, Sachin Keni and Ajay Kawa.

  • All invoices checked vs contract rates
  • Overcharges disputed immediately by Sachin Keni
  • Rebooking incentive reconciled with Lalitha Krishna
  • Tour profitability vs plan calculated
  • Final P&L to Nishanth D and Sachin Keni
  • Ajay Kawa reviews quarterly finance summary
06.3 · Passenger Feedback
NPS & Satisfaction

Structured feedback collected from all passengers within 7 days of return. NPS tracked per tour. Formal complaints responded to within 14 days.

  • Automated feedback survey: WhatsApp and email
  • NPS score per tour tracked over rolling 12 months
  • Hotel and TM ratings captured separately
  • Complaints: formal response within 14 days
  • 9–10 NPS passengers: repeat booking offer
  • Seema and Lalitha to receive monthly NPS summary
06.4 · Monthly Review
Operations Review Cadence

Monthly review of all completed tours. Systemic issues escalated to Sachin Keni and Ajay Kawa. Annual debrief drives next season's contracting and product decisions.

  • Monthly: Clinton, Nishanth D, File Handlers
  • Agenda: open issues, QC failures, supplier performance
  • Quarterly: Sachin Keni, Nishanth D, Clinton — commercial
  • Quarterly: Ajay Kawa receives strategic summary
  • Annual: full season debrief, contract renewal decisions
  • Minutes circulated within 48hrs of each session
