Phase 1 · Strategic Foundation
Pre-Sale — Market Strategy & Product Development

Before a tour is ever sold, GIT must have the product, the rates, and the market reach in place. This phase covers the decisions made 6–12 months before any tour departure — pricing strategy, local field sales, and marketing infrastructure. Currently underdeveloped; scaling this phase is critical to GIT's growth.

Tour Design & Costing

02
Phase 2 · Supply & Commercial
Contracting — Fixed Rates & Committed Supply

The single biggest driver of operational chaos is the absence of pre-contracted, fixed-rate supply. Tours are being priced on the basis of hoped-for spot rates, and components are booked last-minute to "save money." This must stop. All key suppliers must be contracted annually on fixed terms, with allotments released per departure. This phase must be completed by October for the following summer season.

Phase Owner
Nishant + Sachin
GIT Head + Supply
02.1 · Hotels
Fixed Hotel Contracts

Negotiate and sign fixed allotment contracts with all European hotels used across GIT itineraries. No ad-hoc rate shopping during live tours.

  • Annual contract per hotel: fixed net rate, release policy
  • Allotment: minimum 2 rooms per departure date
  • Child and single supplement agreed upfront
  • Signed contracts in place by October each year
  • Ken Newton scope: 24 core European hotels
Oct deadline
02.2 · Transport
Coach & Transfer Contracts

Fix coach rates with Adonis and key European transport providers per route and season. Eliminate ad-hoc quoting during live operational periods.

  • Rate per km/day agreed by route (UK, France, Benelux, Alps)
  • Max 45 passengers per vehicle — contractual cap
  • Dual-coach rate for groups exceeding 45
  • Driver + TM room costs included in rate
  • 20-day notice clause for allocation changes
Oct deadline
02.3 · Sightseeing
Attraction Pre-Allotments

Pre-purchase group ticket allotments for high-demand attractions. No paying walk-up prices or being turned away at peak venues.

  • Disney Paris: group portal booking, not individual tickets
  • Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Colosseum: group entry agreements
  • Pink Strawberries / local DMCs for specialist access
  • Allotments released 90 days before departure
  • Cancellation terms: max 14 days without penalty
Oct deadline
02.4 · Tour Managers
TM Panel & Scheduling

Maintain a contracted panel of experienced European TMs. No scrambling for staff when a departure is confirmed.

  • Retain minimum 8 TMs across UK and Europe
  • Budget: 1 TM per 3 tours operated (ORN Connect model)
  • TM briefing pack prepared by ops team T-7
  • TM allocated to tour at T-30, confirmed at T-20
  • Emergency TM cover protocol: Adonis + local contacts
Year-round panel
Critical rule: Once a supply contract is in place, no GIT team member may book outside that contract without written sign-off from the GIT Head and CCO. The practice of delaying bookings to "shop for better rates" is prohibited during the live booking window (T-45 to T-0). All spot-rate decisions must be made before tour launch.
03
Phase 3 · Core Operations — The Critical Phase
Post-Sale — From Booking to Pre-Departure

This is where the majority of current failures occur. Once a passenger books, the clock starts. Every component must be booked, confirmed, and quality-checked before departure — not on the day. Clinton Thomas owns this phase end-to-end, with dedicated file handler accountability per tour and a formal QC function modelled on the Australian standard.

Phase Owner
Clinton Thomas
GIT Ops Lead + QC
03.1 · Booking Receipt
File Creation & Assignment

Every new booking triggers the creation of a tour file. A single named file handler is assigned immediately and owns all components until departure.

  • File created in system within 24hrs of booking receipt
  • File handler named on file — no shared ownership
  • Passenger manifest: name, passport, visa status logged
  • Departure date, itinerary version, group size noted
  • Deposit receipt confirmed, balance due date set
Clinton Thomas oversight
03.2 · Passenger Management
Manifest & Documentation

Maintain a live, accurate passenger manifest from booking through to departure. Visa-pending passengers tracked separately — their components are held until visa confirmed.

  • Live manifest updated with every booking change
  • Visa status tracked: confirmed / pending / refused
  • Refused visa: full refund per policy, file closed
  • Balance collection: T-60 (full payment required)
  • Special requirements: dietary, medical, mobility flagged
File handler owned
03.3 · Internal Handoffs
Department Coordination

Each department — Flights, Hotels, Transport, Sightseeing — receives the confirmed manifest and executes their component bookings per the T-minus schedule. No component is booked ad-hoc.

  • Flights team: passenger sectors confirmed at T-45
  • Hotels team: room allocation released at T-45
  • Transport: Adonis notified directly at T-20
  • Sightseeing: all tickets booked at T-14
  • File handler tracks every component status daily
4-department model
✉
File Handler Rule

One person owns each tour file start to finish. They are the single point of contact for agents, passengers, and internal teams. They cannot delegate accountability — only tasks.

✓
Australian Model Applied

Sahil's team in Australia runs pre-departure checks 2–3 weeks out. Every component is rechecked and confirmed. This is now the GIT Europe standard under Clinton Thomas.

⚠
Visa-Pending Protocol

Passengers with pending visas are tracked separately. All other confirmed pax bookings proceed at T-14 regardless. Visa-pending slots are held, not lost.

⊙
No Last-Minute Booking

Any component not booked by its T-minus deadline triggers an immediate escalation to Clinton Thomas. Zero tolerance for "we'll book it on the day."

05
Phase 5 · Live Operations
On-Tour — Execution & Exception Management

If the T-minus countdown is executed correctly, Phase 5 should be low-drama. The Tour Manager runs the tour; Clinton Thomas is reachable from India during UK office hours; Adonis handles transport; hotels have confirmed rooming lists. The protocols below govern what happens when something nevertheless goes wrong.

On-Ground Owner
Tour Manager
Supported by Clinton Thomas (India)
05.1 · Daily Check-In
TM Daily Reporting

Tour Manager sends a brief daily update to Clinton Thomas — no more than 5 lines. Confirms next day's schedule, any issues, and coach/hotel status. This creates a live audit trail and catches problems early.

  • Daily WhatsApp/email by 8pm local time
  • Confirms: pax count, hotel, next venue, coach
  • Flags any complaints or incidents immediately
  • Clinton acknowledges within 2 hours (India time)
05.2 · Coach Capacity
Transport Protocol

Absolute maximum 45 passengers per coach at all times. Any change to vehicle allocation mid-tour requires Clinton Thomas approval in advance. Adonis is the primary contact for any transport issue.

  • TM counts pax before boarding every day
  • Luggage capacity checked before departure
  • Vehicle change: Clinton + Adonis 48hr notice minimum
  • No pax left behind — ever. Escalate immediately.
05.3 · Sightseeing
Attraction Access Protocol

All tickets are pre-booked and in the TM pack. TM presents booking references at each venue. No walk-up purchases. If access is refused, escalate to Clinton immediately — do not reroute without approval.

  • Booking references in TM pack (printed + digital)
  • Group entry lanes used at all pre-booked venues
  • If refused: call Clinton first, Adonis second
  • Any itinerary deviation requires Clinton sign-off
Escalation · Level 1
Venue Access Refused
  1. TM calls Clinton Thomas immediately
  2. Clinton contacts venue group bookings line
  3. If unresolved: identify nearest alternative venue
  4. Clinton approves reroute and notifies Adonis
  5. Incident logged; refund claim initiated if applicable
Escalation · Level 2
Hotel Issue (Room / Availability)
  1. TM contacts Clinton Thomas with details
  2. Clinton calls hotel directly on contracted number
  3. If unresolvable: source alternative rooms same location
  4. Cost difference: hotel bears, not GIT
  5. Nishant notified; incident report filed within 24hrs
Escalation · Level 3
Medical / Passenger Emergency
  1. TM calls emergency services (112 Europe-wide)
  2. Immediate call to Clinton Thomas
  3. Clinton contacts passenger's insurance provider
  4. Nishant & Raj notified within 30 minutes
  5. Agent and family contacted by India office

Phase 6 · Continuous Improvement
Post-Tour — Review, Learn, Improve

Every tour is a data point. The post-tour review closes the loop — capturing what worked, what didn't, and what needs to change before the next departure. This feeds directly back into Phase 1 (product design) and Phase 2 (contracting), making each season better than the last.

Review Owner
Clinton + Nishant
Reported to Raj / Alia
06.1 · Debrief
TM Debrief Report

Tour Manager submits a structured debrief within 5 days of tour completion. This is non-negotiable and forms the official record of the tour.

  • Hotels: quality, cleanliness, rooming issues
  • Transport: coach quality, driver professionalism
  • Sightseeing: access, timing, pax satisfaction
  • Incidents: any complaints, escalations, resolutions
  • Pax sentiment: overall satisfaction score (1–10)
06.2 · Finance Close
Cost Reconciliation

Finance team closes the tour file within 10 days — reconciling all supplier invoices against contracted rates. Variances investigated and escalated.

  • All supplier invoices checked vs contract rates
  • Overcharges disputed immediately
  • Rebooking incentive reconciled (Lalitha / Seema)
  • Tour profitability vs plan calculated
  • Final P&L shared with Nishant and Clinton
06.3 · Passenger Feedback
NPS & Satisfaction

Structured feedback collected from all passengers within 7 days of return. Results feed directly into product improvement and agent performance tracking.

  • Automated feedback survey (WhatsApp / email)
  • NPS score per tour tracked over time
  • Hotel and TM ratings captured
  • Complaints: formal response within 14 days
  • Repeat booking offer for 9–10 NPS passengers
06.4 · Monthly Review
Operations Review Meeting

Monthly review of all completed tours. Issues escalated to Raj and Alia quarterly. Systemic problems feed into next season's contracting and product decisions.

  • Monthly: Clinton, Nishant, File Handlers
  • Agenda: open issues, QC failures, supplier performance
  • Quarterly: Raj, Alia, Clinton — strategic review
  • Annual: full season debrief, contract renewal decisions
  • Minutes circulated within 48hrs of each meeting
